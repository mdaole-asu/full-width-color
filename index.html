Pat Tillman Veterans Center
 
 
%%First Name%%,

Thank you for deciding to apply to Arizona State University! We hope you find that ASU is the right place for you to receive your degree.

ASU is highly ranked for its service to military and veteran students, and the Pat Tillman Veterans Center is here to help you be successful. Since most of us in the center are veterans, we understand the sacrifice you have made and are grateful for your service to our country. I serve as the veteran recruitment specialist, and I am here to help you navigate the various admission processes at ASU.
 
Have you applied for your VA education benefits?
If you have not yet applied for your VA benefits, please go to the VONAPP on the VA’s website to apply. Using Firefox as your internet browser to fill out the VONAPP is not recommended. You should apply at least 90 days before the first day of classes to receive your benefits at the start of the semester. (Classes start Jan. 9, 2017, so you should apply no later than Oct. 11, 2016.)
Upon admission to ASU, you will need to complete a few additional items for the Pat Tillman Veterans Center to ensure you receive your benefits.

Learn more about your benefits. ⟶
 
We’re all about veteran success
ASU is one of only a few universities with an on-site representative of the Veteran Affairs’ VetSuccess on Campus program. ASU’s representative can help you make the most of your academic experience at ASU whether or not you are eligible for education benefits.
A new chapter of VA benefits, Chapter 36, is an opportunity to discuss your employment goals and degree choice with a VetSuccess Counselor. Please contact ASU’s VetSuccess Counselor Troy Rundle at Troy.Rundle@va.gov to see how he can assist you.

Read more about VetSuccess on Campus. ⟶
 
We believe in you
I look forward to helping you get answers to your questions about applying, admission, initial enrollment or benefits. Visit veterans.asu.edu to view our newsletters and all the events, opportunities and activities you can look forward to when you’re admitted to ASU.
Sincerely,
